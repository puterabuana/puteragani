How to Actually Sell These Extensions (4 Proven Systems)

Having a monetizable extension is step 1. Selling it is step 2. Here are 4 battle-tested systems that work for most of these extensions. Pick one, master it, then layer on the others.

System 1: Service Retainer Model (Recurring Revenue)

🔄 Best For: Gmail, Google Docs, Slack, Translation
Charge Monthly Subscription for Ongoing Management
Client pays £200-500/month. You maintain the integration, optimize it, add features, provide support. Revenue is recurring, predictable, scales beautifully. Example: Gmail email management service = £350/month × 15 customers = £5.25K/month.
1Create a landing page: Show 1 problem you solve. "Cut email management time from 2 hours/day to 15 minutes."
2Target the right audience: Salespeople, executives, founders. Find them on LinkedIn. Send 20 personalized messages: "I help salespeople get through email 80% faster using ChatGPT."
3Offer discovery call: No pitch. Just ask questions. Understand their email pain. Then propose: "£350/month for a complete email system. I'll handle everything."
4Close with guarantees: "If you don't love it in 30 days, 100% refund." Removes risk. Increases close rate from 5% to 15%.
5Onboard & deliver: 2-hour setup. Then hands-off management (VA can handle 50+ clients). Collect testimonials after 60 days of success.
💰 Math: 20 pitches → 1 customer (5% close rate). 1 customer = £350/month = £4.2K/year recurring. By customer #10: £35K/year passive recurring. That's life-changing income.

System 2: Done-For-You Project Model (High Ticket)

🎯 Best For: Web Browsing research, Zapier automation, Translation
Charge £2K-5K Per Project (5-10 Hours Work)
Instead of monthly subscriptions, charge per project. Custom research report? £3.5K. Zapier automation workflow? £2.5K. Translation package? £1.5K per 100K words. Higher ticket, lower volume = less sales needed.
1
Build portfolio: First 1-2 projects at 50% off (£1K-1.5K instead of £2.5K-3K). You need proof. Worth it.
2List on marketplaces: Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal. Title: "Custom Market Research Reports Using AI" or "ChatGPT + Zapier Automation Setup."
3Pitch your ideal client directly: Find venture capital firms, business development managers, companies expanding. Email: "I specialize in custom competitive analysis. Want a free sample report on your top 5 competitors?"
4Deliver insanely good work: Over-deliver on first project. Make client look like a hero. They refer 2-3 people. Now you have inbound.
5Raise prices with each client: Client #1: £2K. Client #2: £2.5K. Client #3: £3K. Client #4: £3.5K. By client #5, you're at full price.
💰 Math: 2 projects/month at average £2.5K = £5K/month recurring. 4 projects/month = £10K/month. Once you hit 2 per week (8/month), you're at £20K/month and can hire a team.

System 3: Digital Product Model (Passive Income)

📦 Best For: DALL-E templates, Gmail workflows, Slack bots
Build Templates/Workflows. Sell Unlimited Times. Zero Delivery Work.
Create once, sell infinite times. "ChatGPT Email Automation Template for Salespeople" = £29 on Gumroad. Sell 100/month = £2.9K. Sell 500/month = £14.5K. No delivery. Pure margins.
1Create the product: Template, guide, checklist, framework. How-to guide for setting up ChatGPT + the extension. Include screenshots, videos, step-by-step instructions.
2Price intelligently: £19-49 (most people buy). Bundle 3 templates = £99 (higher perceived value). Monthly subscription for unlimited templates = £9-19/month.
3Launch on distribution: Product Hunt (100-200 upvotes = 500 sales). Twitter thread (sell via link in bio). Reddit communities. Email list if you have one.
4Optimize based on feedback: What questions do people ask? Add that to the guide. Make it better. Price goes up as quality improves.
5Create complementary products: First product = basic template (£29). Second = advanced version (£79). Third = bundle (£99). Now single customer buys multiple products.
💰 Math: 50 sales/month × £29 = £1.45K/month (passive!). 100 sales/month = £2.9K. 300 sales/month = £8.7K. Scale via ads or partnerships to accelerate.

System 4: White-Label / Affiliate Model (Leverage)

🤝 Best For: Any extension (most scalable)
Partner With Agencies. They Sell, You Build. 40-50% Revenue Split.
Don't sell yourself. Partner with 5-10 agencies. They have clients. They sell your service. You build/maintain it. You get 40-50% of revenue. They get 50-60%. Everyone wins. You go from sales grind to product/delivery focus.
1Build replicable system: Make your service so repeatable that agencies can offer it to clients without much explanation. Templates, scripts, playbooks.
2Find agencies: Marketing agencies, automation agencies, business consulting firms. They have 10-50 clients. Perfect distribution.
3Pitch partnership: "I deliver £3K/month email services for your clients. You charge £5K, keep £2K, I keep £3K. You get new revenue stream, I get distribution."
4Close deal & execute: Agency pitches to their clients. You deliver. Get testimonials. Now agency has case studies to sell more.
5Scale to 10 agencies: 10 agencies × 2 customers each × £3K/month = £60K/month revenue. You just deliver (team can help). No sales needed after initial 10 partnerships.
💰 Math: Partner with 5 agencies. Each brings 1-2 customers. You get 40% of revenue = 5-10 customers × £3K × 40% = £6K-12K/month. By month 12, you're at 20-30 customers via agency partnerships = £24K-36K/month with minimal sales effort.
⚠️ Reality Check: First Customer Determines Your Path

Your first paying customer shapes your business. If it's a retainer customer, you're in System 1 (good recurring revenue). If it's a project, you're in System 2 (higher ticket, lower volume). Pick the system that fits your personality, then double down on it for 6 months before adding others.
